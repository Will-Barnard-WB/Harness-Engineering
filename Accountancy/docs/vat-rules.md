# UK VAT Rules

Last updated: March 2026

## Rates

| Rate     | %   | Common examples |
|----------|-----|-----------------|
| Standard | 20% | Most goods and services, electronics, clothing (adult), restaurants |
| Reduced  | 5%  | Domestic fuel and power, children's car seats, sanitary products, mobility aids |
| Zero     | 0%  | Food (most), books and newspapers, children's clothing, public transport, medicine |
| Exempt   | n/a | Financial services, education, health services, insurance, postage stamps |

## VAT number format

UK VAT numbers must match: `GB` followed by exactly 9 digits.
Example: `GB 123 4567 89` or `GB123456789` (spaces optional).

Any other format is invalid and must be flagged.

## Mixed supplies

A single receipt may contain items at different rates.
Each line item must be assessed individually — do not apply one rate to the whole receipt.

## Input tax reclaim

Businesses can reclaim input VAT on purchases used for business purposes.
The following categories carry reclaim risk and must always be flagged:

- Food and drink consumed on premises or taken away (staff entertainment)
- Alcohol of any kind
- Client entertainment
- Items described as personal (toiletries, clothing for personal use)
- Gym or health club memberships

## Registration threshold

As of 2026, businesses must register for VAT once taxable turnover exceeds £90,000.
Below this threshold, VAT registration is optional.
