# Progress Summary — Writing Guidelines

This document specifies how to write the student's yearly progress paragraph once
the teacher has provided the three ratings below.

## Teacher Ratings

The harness collects three ratings from the teacher, each on a scale of 1–5:

| Rating              | 1          | 2       | 3        | 4       | 5             |
|---------------------|------------|---------|----------|---------|---------------|
| Effort              | Very poor  | Poor    | Adequate | Good    | Exceptional   |
| Class Engagement    | Disengaged | Passive | Moderate | Active  | Outstanding   |
| Quality of Work     | Very weak  | Weak    | Adequate | Strong  | Excellent     |

## Language Mapping

Use the language below when describing each rating. Do not use the number or the
label directly in the paragraph — weave the meaning into natural prose.

### Effort
| Score | Language guidance |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | "has struggled to demonstrate consistent effort throughout the year" |
| 2 | "has not always applied themselves as fully as their potential allows" |
| 3 | "has shown a satisfactory level of effort across the year" |
| 4 | "has worked diligently and shown a strong commitment to their studies" |
| 5 | "has demonstrated exceptional dedication and a consistently strong work ethic" |

### Class Engagement
| Score | Language guidance |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | "rarely contributed to classroom discussions or activities" |
| 2 | "tended to work quietly and did not often engage with wider class discussions" |
| 3 | "participated in class activities at a reasonable level" |
| 4 | "engaged actively and thoughtfully in lessons, contributing meaningfully to discussions" |
| 5 | "was an outstanding contributor to the learning environment, consistently enriching class discussions" |

### Quality of Work
| Score | Language guidance |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | "the quality of submitted work was generally very weak and requires significant development" |
| 2 | "work submitted showed some understanding but lacked depth and accuracy in key areas" |
| 3 | "produced work of an adequate standard that meets the basic requirements of the course" |
| 4 | "consistently produced strong work that demonstrated a clear understanding of course content" |
| 5 | "produced excellent work throughout the year, often displaying insight beyond the requirements of the course" |

## Paragraph Structure

The summary must follow this structure:
1. **Opening sentence**: Name the student and give an overall impression of their year (positive, mixed, or needs support) — do not mention the grade
2. **Effort + engagement** (1–2 sentences): blend the effort and class engagement ratings
3. **Quality of work** (1 sentence): use the quality language mapping
4. **Forward-looking close** (1 sentence): an encouraging and specific statement about next steps or continued development

## Rules

- Write in third person ("Sophie has...", not "You have...")
- 4–5 sentences total — no more, no less
- Do not mention numerical scores or grades in the paragraph
- Do not copy the language mapping verbatim — adapt it to flow naturally
- Tone must be professional and appropriate for a school report: encouraging but honest
- If all three ratings are 3 or above, the tone should be positive
- If any rating is 1 or 2, acknowledge the challenge while maintaining a constructive tone
