# Feedback Guidelines — Student Reviews

## Purpose

These guidelines apply to all written content in student reviews. They take
precedence over general writing instincts — follow them exactly.

## Audience

The review is read by:
1. The student
2. The student's parents or guardians
3. The school's pastoral and academic records system

All three audiences must be considered. Assume the student will read it.

## Tone

- Professional, warm, and constructive
- Never sarcastic, dismissive, or excessively effusive
- Specific where possible — vague praise ("a good student") is not helpful
- Avoid jargon or overly formal language; this is a school report, not a legal document

## Language Rules

- Third person throughout ("Sophie", "she", "her" — never "you" or "I")
- British English spelling (behaviour, programme, recognise, fulfil)
- Avoid clichés: "a pleasure to teach", "blossomed", "goes from strength to strength"
- Do not mention specific assessment scores or percentage marks in the summary paragraph
- Do not mention the predicted grade in the paragraph — it appears in the review header

## Grade Commentary (in review header only)

When noting the predicted grade in the review header:
- A* / A: "on track for an excellent outcome"
- B: "on track for a strong outcome"
- C: "on track for a satisfactory outcome"
- D / E: "working towards a pass; continued focus is important"
- U: "currently below pass threshold; immediate support is recommended"

## Length

- Summary paragraph: 4–5 sentences only
- Do not pad with filler sentences to reach length targets
- Do not truncate to avoid delivering honest feedback

## What to Avoid

- Speculation about personal circumstances ("perhaps due to difficulties at home...")
- Comparisons with other students ("one of the strongest in the class")
- Absolute statements that can't be substantiated ("will definitely achieve...")
- Negative framing that offers no path forward ("simply doesn't try")
